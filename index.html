I am Corbin Long, a student of Web & Computer Programming as well as a pizza connoisseur who enjoys trail running, hiking, traveling, playing the electric guitar and conducting genealogical research. I am currently enrolled in the WDD 130 Web Fundamentals course where I am learning the basics of creating webpages with HTML (Hypertext Markup Language) and CSS (Cascading Style Sheets). I feel like I have learned so much about HTML and CSS throughout this course and I am grateful to be able to learn these concepts. In a way, I view creating webpages as a form of art, a very technical form that is. My favorite pizza is New York Style (pepperoni, plain chesse and margherita), Detroit Style Deep-Dish (pepperoni and supreme), Chicago Style Deep Dish (pepperoni and sausage) and, Neopolitan Margherita (Original Italian Style). Similar to what I said about creating webpages with HTML and CSS, making pizza is very much a form of art as well. However, pizza making, is more of a scientific form of art which is ultilized throughout the techniques and methods of preparing the dough to rise as well as baking it at the right temperature in proportion to the length of baking. The higher the temperature of the oven is, the shorter amount of time needed to bake the pizza will be.
